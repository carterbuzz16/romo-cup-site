Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about The Romo Cup, the tournament, and the mission behind it.

General

Start here.

What is The Romo Cup?

The Romo Cup is an annual Auburn charity wiffle ball tournament created to honor Will “Romo” Romanowski through competition, community, and purpose. The event brings together fraternities, families, and supporters while raising money for the Auburn University endowed scholarship created in his name.

Who was Will “Romo” Romanowski?

Will Romanowski, known to many as Romo, was someone deeply loved by his friends, family, and the Auburn community. The Romo Cup was created to honor his life, his impact, and the spirit he brought to the people around him.

When is The Romo Cup?

The tournament is scheduled for April 25, 2026.

Where is the event held?

Tournament locations and game details are shared on the bracket and event pages. Opening games and championship games may take place at different fraternity fields depending on the schedule.

Tournament

How the day works.

Who competes in The Romo Cup?

Auburn fraternities compete in the tournament, coming together not just for competition, but to support a meaningful cause tied to the Auburn community.

How does the tournament format work?

The Romo Cup uses an 8-team double elimination format, which means teams have more than one path through the tournament before a champion is crowned.

Where can I see the bracket?

The live bracket page on the site shows matchups, tournament progression, and the event schedule.

Can people come watch the games?

Yes. Supporters, friends, family, and members of the Auburn community are encouraged to follow along and support the event.
