The Story of Romo

A life remembered through hope, encouragement, and a community committed to being better than yesterday.

William “Romo” Romanowski

William Romanowski, known as Romo, left behind more than memories. He left behind words that gave his family strength in one of the hardest seasons of their lives.

A note written by Will became a source of comfort and direction. While deeply personal, its message was clear: keep encouraging one another, keep showing up for people, and keep moving forward with purpose. That spirit now lives on through the Auburn University endowed scholarship, the Will "Romo" Scholarship, awarded each year to an Auburn student.

"Choose encouragement. Keep going. Be better than yesterday."

In honor of Will, his family created inspirational bracelets to share that same encouragement with others. Each bracelet is a reminder that hope can be carried, even through grief.

From a Note to a Mission

What began as a private message became a public mission. The bracelets opened a way to comfort others while keeping Will’s spirit present in everyday life.

The Romo Cup grew from that same heartbeat. It is a day where competition, connection, and generosity come together to honor Will’s legacy and support the Auburn University endowed scholarship, the Will "Romo" Scholarship.

Every team, every supporter, and every donation helps turn memory into impact by strengthening community and funding an annual scholarship recipient: an Auburn student selected for the Will "Romo" Scholarship at Auburn University.
