Who Was Will?

Will “Romo” Romanowski was easy-going, quick to laugh, and the kind of person who made people feel welcome immediately. He was a star student with a 4.05 GPA, a scholarship recipient at Auburn, and a proud Sigma Nu fraternity member.

He loved baseball from every angle, from youth leagues to travel ball, from cheering for the Phillies to backing Auburn baseball. He dreamed of one day working in a Major League Baseball front office.

After the accident, a note from Will was found and it became the heart of his legacy. Its spirit now drives this entire movement.

“Be Better Than Yesterday.”

The Romo Cup exists to permanently fund the Will Romanowski Endowed Scholarship and carry Will’s values forward.

What Happened?

Will tragically passed away in a car accident after his freshman year at Auburn.

The loss was felt deeply by his family, his friends, Sigma Nu, and the broader Auburn community.

How Did The Romo Cup Begin?

It started simply: a whiffle ball game at Sigma Nu, a table flipped on its side, and “BBTY” written across it.

A few fraternity leaders took that spark and formalized it into an official event. What began as one game grew into a multi-fraternity tournament in Will’s honor.

Why whiffle ball? Because Will loved baseball. Because it brings people together. Because it captures fun, friendship, and competition in the exact spirit he lived with.

Why We Play

The mission is clear: raise $26,000+ to establish and fund the Will Romanowski Endowed Scholarship at Auburn University.

This cements Will’s name in Auburn’s legacy, lightens the financial burden for a student, and shares Will’s story with every recipient.

This is about more than money. It is about uniting the Greek community. It is about honoring legacy. It is about living “Be Better Than Yesterday.”

Who Will Receive the Scholarship?

The Will Romanowski Endowed Scholarship is awarded annually to an Auburn student.

It is designed to support a deserving student who embodies leadership, character, and community.

Every award keeps Will’s legacy alive for generations of Auburn students to come.
